# Tech Intelligence Notes

This repository is a learning experiment in building an AI-assisted research system (and shareable skills) that:

1. Ingests new content on a recurring basis (content collection),
2. Converts that content into selective daily signal (structured interpretation),
3. Updates a slower-moving thesis / strategy document as evidence accumulates (ongoing thesis review)

The immediate source for Phase 1 is Techmeme, but the broader goal is to develop a reusable workflow for multipe content inputs. 

The broader goal is to explore how to build an AI system that can:
- ingest content regularly,
- make selective judgments rather than merely summarize,
- preserve memory over time,
- update a thesis or strategy document without overreacting to daily noise,
- and become a useful thinking partner for research, investing, and product strategy.

## What this repository does

This repo is designed around a two-layer model:
- **Daily notes** are closer to the news flow and record new evidence.
- **Synthesized implications** act as the durable memory and strategy layer.

### 1. Daily notes
Daily notes capture the most important developments from the source feed in a selective, business- and capability-focused format.

They are meant to answer:
- What changed?
- Why does it matter?
- What might it imply?

The emphasis is not on comprehensive news recap. It is on extracting developments that matter for:
- markets and macro-sensitive sectors
- industry structure and competition
- investment watchlists
- startup opportunities
- product strategy

### 2. Synthesized implications
The synthesized implications document is the slower-moving thesis / strategy layer.

It is meant to:
- identify durable themes,
- track whether those themes are strengthening or weakening,
- record contradictory evidence,
- and maintain a more stable view of what appears strategically important.

It is not a second daily summary.
It should update conservatively and only when recent evidence justifies a change.
