That makes post-quantum migration look more like an active roadmap problem than a distant science project. [Reinforcing]

## 2026-04-02

### Capability / technology shifts
- Alibaba Cloud launched Qwen3.6-Plus with a 1M context window and said it had drastically enhanced agentic coding, after releasing two other proprietary models in the prior three days. Coding-model competition still looks broader and faster-moving than the market narrative around a few US labs suggests. [Reinforcing]
- Cloudflare launched EmDash, an open-source TypeScript CMS built on Astro with sandboxed plugins, built-in MCP, and x402 support, and said AI coding agents helped it rebuild Next.js in a week. If platform vendors can ship full application stacks this quickly, more of the software market may get contested by companies that used to stop at infrastructure. [Watch]

### Strategic / market implications
- Sources told The Economic Times that Oracle cut around 10,000 jobs in India as part of a global restructuring that reportedly affects about 30,000 employees. AI capex is starting to look big enough to force labor tradeoffs even at mature software and cloud companies, not just at frontier labs. [Reinforcing]
- Reuters cited IDC data showing Chinese GPU and AI chipmakers captured about 41% of China's AI accelerator server market in 2025, eroding Nvidia's share to 55%. Export controls and industrial policy are not just constraining supply; they are starting to reallocate share toward domestic compute stacks. [Reinforcing]
- Reuters reported that AWS's Bahrain operation was damaged after an Iranian strike, after AWS had already disclosed disruption in the region during the current conflict. Even when cloud infrastructure is not the intended target, frontier compute now sits inside normal geopolitical and physical-risk maps. [Reinforcing]

Leading labs may start treating owned media and narrative distribution as part of product and policy strategy, but one deal is still not enough to call this a durable advantage. [Watch]

## 2026-04-04

### Capability / technology shifts
- Reuters reported DeepSeek's V4 will run on Huawei chips after DeepSeek worked with Huawei and Cambricon to adapt parts of the model's base code. Hardware fit is becoming part of model development, not just a deployment choice after the model is done. [Reinforcing]

### Strategic / market implications
- Anthropic said Claude subscriptions will stop covering third-party harnesses like OpenClaw because those usage patterns were not what the plans were built for. Flat-rate AI pricing looks less stable once agentic products start looping heavily, and vendors may reserve subsidized access for their own products and API. [Reinforcing]
- Reuters reported Alibaba, ByteDance, and Tencent placed large orders for Huawei chips ahead of DeepSeek V4. Export controls are not just limiting China; they are helping force a tighter domestic stack across model labs, chip vendors, and cloud buyers. [Reinforcing]
- Wired reported Meta paused work with Mercor after a LiteLLM-linked breach, and OpenAI said it is investigating the incident. AI labs now have a real supply-chain security problem around data vendors, not just around models and chips. [Reinforcing]
- Bloomberg reported Microsoft hit internal Copilot sales goals after shifting its sales strategy, but only 3% of customers were paying for Copilot as of January. Even the strongest software distribution may not turn AI into paid seats without heavy selling, which may point to a missing hypothesis around forced monetization versus real pull. [Watch]

For now, AI still seems to raise demand for engineers who can deploy and supervise systems faster than it reduces headcount, which reinforces the view that implementation demand may stay strong before automation fully changes labor markets.
